ByeTunes is a native iOS app that lets you add music directly to your Apple Music library — no Mac, no iTunes, no syncing. By communicating with the iOS media database over a local connection, ByeTunes gives you full control over your music library from your iPhone itself.

## What ByeTunes does

ByeTunes works by connecting to your iPhone over a local VPN tunnel, reading and writing directly to the iOS media database (`MediaLibrary.sqlitedb`) and iTunes Control file system. When you inject a song, ByeTunes:

1. Copies the audio file to the device's iTunes music folder via AFC
2. Builds a properly structured database entry with all metadata
3. Triggers a sync notification so Apple Music picks up the new tracks immediately

## Get started in four steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install LocalDevVPN">
    Download [LocalDevVPN](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/localdevvpn/id6755608044) from the App Store and tap **Connect** to create the local tunnel ByeTunes needs.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate a pairing file">
    Use [idevice_pair](https://github.com/jkcoxson/idevice_pair) on your computer to generate a pairing file, then AirDrop or share it to your iPhone.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Import the pairing file into ByeTunes">
    On first launch, ByeTunes shows the pairing screen. Tap **Import Pairing File** and select the `.plist` file you generated.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add and inject music">
    Tap **Add Songs**, pick your audio files from the Files app, then tap **Inject to Device**. Your music appears in Apple Music immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Supported formats

| Type | Formats |
|------|---------|
| Music | MP3, M4A, FLAC, WAV, AIFF |
| Ringtones | M4R |

<Note>
  ByeTunes requires iOS 16.0 or later. iOS 26.4 and newer require an RP Pairing File instead of a regular pairing file — see the [iOS 26.4+ setup guide](/setup/ios264-rp-pairing) for details.
</Note>

---
title: "Inject your first song into Apple Music with ByeTunes"
description: "A complete walkthrough from installing LocalDevVPN to importing a pairing file and injecting your first audio file into your iOS Apple Music library."
---

This guide walks you through the full setup flow and your first music injection. The one-time setup takes about 10–15 minutes; after that, adding songs takes seconds.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Install LocalDevVPN on your iPhone">
    ByeTunes communicates with the iOS media database over a local VPN tunnel. LocalDevVPN creates that tunnel.

    Download [LocalDevVPN](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/localdevvpn/id6755608044) from the App Store (also available on AltStore PAL). Once installed, open it and tap **Connect**. Keep LocalDevVPN connected whenever you use ByeTunes.

    <Note>
      LocalDevVPN must be actively connected before you import a pairing file or inject music. If the VPN is disconnected, ByeTunes cannot reach your device.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Install ByeTunes">
    ByeTunes is distributed as a sideloaded IPA — it is not available on the App Store. Install it using AltStore, Signulous, Sideloadly, or another signing method before continuing.

    See the [installation guide](/installation) for step-by-step instructions for each signing service.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Generate a pairing file on your computer">
    ByeTunes needs a pairing file to authenticate with your device. You generate this once from your Mac or PC.

    1. Download and run [idevice_pair](https://github.com/jkcoxson/idevice_pair) on your computer with your iPhone connected via USB.
    2. Follow the prompts to generate the pairing file (a `.plist` file).
    3. Transfer the file to your iPhone using AirDrop, iCloud Drive, or any other method.

    For a detailed walkthrough, see [Pairing file setup](/setup/pairing-file).

    <Note>
      If your iPhone is running **iOS 26.4 or later**, you need an RP Pairing File instead of a standard pairing file. See [iOS 26.4+ RP Pairing](/setup/ios264-rp-pairing) for instructions.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Import the pairing file into ByeTunes">
    Open ByeTunes. On first launch you will see the onboarding screen asking you to import your pairing file.

    1. Confirm LocalDevVPN is connected.
    2. Tap **Import Pairing File**.
    3. Navigate to the `.plist` file you transferred in the previous step and select it.

    ByeTunes will connect to your device and display "Successfully Connected!" once the pairing is verified. The onboarding screen disappears and the main interface loads.

    <Tip>
      If you installed ByeTunes through a signing service like AltStore or Signulous, the app uses `asCopy: true` when presenting the file picker. This is intentional — it prevents a crash that can occur with security-scoped bookmarks in signed apps.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add songs to the queue">
    Tap **Add Songs** in the Music tab. The Files app picker opens — navigate to your audio files and select them. ByeTunes supports MP3, M4A, FLAC, WAV, and AIFF.

    After selecting files, ByeTunes reads each file's metadata and artwork. You can review or edit the information before injecting.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Inject songs to your Apple Music library">
    Once your songs are in the queue, tap **Inject to Device**. ByeTunes copies each file to the device's iTunes Control folder, writes the database entries, and notifies Apple Music.

    When injection completes, open Apple Music — your tracks will be there.
  </Step>
</Steps>

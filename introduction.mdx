ByeTunes is a native iOS app for anyone tired of relying on iTunes or Finder to manage their music. Instead of syncing through a computer, ByeTunes communicates directly with the iOS media database over a local VPN tunnel, letting you add songs from your iPhone itself — no cable, no desktop app, no sync cycle.

## Who it's for

ByeTunes is built for people who want full control over their Apple Music library without being locked into Apple's sync workflow. If you have audio files you want to play natively in Apple Music — whether that's lossless FLAC rips, MP3 downloads, or personal recordings — ByeTunes gets them there without a computer in the loop.

## Supported formats

ByeTunes accepts the following audio formats for music injection:

| Type | Formats |
|------|---------|
| Music | MP3, M4A, FLAC, WAV, AIFF |
| Ringtones | M4R |

## Requirements

- **iOS 16.0 or later** — ByeTunes will not run on earlier versions.
- **LocalDevVPN** — required on the device to establish the local tunnel ByeTunes uses to connect.
- **A pairing file** — generated once from your computer using [idevice_pair](https://github.com/jkcoxson/idevice_pair).

<Note>
  iOS 26.4 and later require an RP Pairing File rather than a standard pairing file. If you update your device to iOS 26.4+, ByeTunes will prompt you to re-import the new file. See the [iOS 26.4+ RP Pairing guide](/setup/ios264-rp-pairing) for details.
</Note>

## How it works under the hood

ByeTunes uses the `idevice` library (a `libimobiledevice` alternative) to communicate with the iOS file system over a local loopback connection provided by LocalDevVPN. When you inject a song, ByeTunes:

1. Copies the audio file into the device's iTunes Control folder via AFC
2. Writes a correctly structured entry to the iOS media database (`MediaLibrary.sqlitedb`)
3. Triggers a media library notification so Apple Music picks up the new tracks immediately

No server is involved, and no data leaves your device.
